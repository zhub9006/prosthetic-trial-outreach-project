## Executive Summary

This assessment evaluates two dimensions of our prosthetic trial outreach project:

1. **Clinical trial gaps** — Analysis of 427+ lower limb prosthetic trials on ClinicalTrials.gov revealing significant underrepresentation of industry-sponsored research (only 19%), racial/ethnic diversity in enrollment, rural access, pediatric populations, and Phase 2–3 efficacy trials (just 5 trials, 1%).

2. **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a community outreach site** — A site-level evaluation showing excellent walkability (10/10) and public transit access (9.4/10), but critical gaps in DME vendors (0/10), social services (0/10), parks (0/10), and accessible parking.

**Key verdict:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is a strong hub for trial operations but requires active outreach expansion to reach underserved prosthetic users.

### Identified Gaps for Underserved Groups

1. **Racial/Ethnic Diversity:** In the 10 trials reviewed, racial/ethnic breakdowns were rarely reported. The only trial with visible demographic data (NCT04427007) enrolled 100% White participants.

2. **Lower-Limb Specificity:** Several trials overlap with upper-limb prosthetics (e.g., REINVENT), and many focus on transtibial only, excluding transfemoral users.

3. **Rural Access:** Most trial sites are in major metropolitan areas (Chicago, Boston, UK, France). No trials are based in the rural Midwest or Southern US.

4. **Age Range:** Many trials restrict enrollment to ages 18-60 or 18-65, excluding older adults who represent a large portion of the amputee population.

5. **Female Representation:** Many trials do not report sex-specific enrollment, and several have all-male or heavily male-skewed cohorts.

6. **Insurance/Financial Barriers:** Industry-sponsored trials may require participants to have specific insurance or travel to distant sites, creating access barriers for low-income populations.

7. **Phase Gap:** 96% of trials are in NA/Unknown phases, meaning very few prosthetic interventions have undergone rigorous later-stage testing before market release.

### Key Accessibility Pros for Prosthetic Users

1. **World-class rehab facility:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab is one of the top rehabilitation hospitals in the US, with dedicated prosthetic and orthotic programs
2. **High walkability (10/10):** Flat, well-maintained sidewalks; accessible building entrances
3. **Extensive public transit (48 stops):** Easy to reach without a car
4. **Multiple 24/7 pharmacies:** Critical for prosthetic users needing ongoing supplies
5. **Divvy bike-share:** Alternative mobility option (adapted cycles may need to be requested)
6. **Abundant dining and amenities:** Supports extended stays for trial participants
7. **Shopping at Michigan Ave:** Within easy reach for daily needs

---

### Key Accessibility Limitations

1. **No parks within 500m:** Outdoor activity/recreation space limited; nearby lakefront is ~1km away
2. **No DME/prosthetic vendors:** Critical gap — prosthetic users must travel elsewhere for supplies, adjustments, and repairs
3. **Limited social services nearby:** No dedicated social work or disability services offices within 500m
4. **Parking is limited and expensive:** Only 4 facilities within range, all fee-based; no ADA-designated spots within 200m
5. **No dedicated transit shelters:** Most CTA stops lack shelters and tactile paving
6. **Urban density:** Streeterville is a high-density commercial/residential area; may be overwhelming for some trial participants
7. **No parks or green space:** Zero park amenities within 500m for outdoor rehabilitation

## Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 1. Community-Based Screening Events

- **Target:** Underrepresented groups identified in the gap analysis (rural populations, older adults, racial/ethnic minorities, low-income individuals)
- **Approach:** Set up mobile screening stations at community centers, churches, and VA facilities in underserved areas
- **AbilityLab as hub:** Use the AbilityLab's reputation and infrastructure to support satellite screening events

### 2. Tele-Trial Participation Program

- **Address the rural access gap:** Develop a hybrid trial model combining in-person visits at the AbilityLab with telehealth check-ins
- **Reduce travel burden:** For participants living >50 miles from Chicago, offer travel stipends and lodging partnerships

### 3. Industry-Academic Partnership Pipeline

- **Address the industry gap:** Create a matching program connecting prosthetic device companies (Össur, Stryker, Ottobock, Liberating Technologies) with underserved trial sites
- **Pro bono device donations:** Negotiate with manufacturers for subsidized or donated devices for trial participants who cannot afford them

### 4. Diversity & Inclusion Benchmarks

- **Mandate demographic reporting:** Require all trial protocols to report racial/ethnic, sex, and age breakdowns of enrollment
- **Set minimum diversity targets:** 30% enrollment from underserved populations for trials receiving AbilityLab support

### 5. Patient Navigation & Transportation Services

- **Dedicated navigator:** Hire a patient navigator to help prosthetic users understand trial eligibility, enroll, and complete study visits
- **Transportation assistance:** Partner with Divvy bike-share (adapted cycles), CTA (reduced-fare cards), and ride-share services for trial participants

### 6. Older Adult & Caregiver-Focused Programming

- **Lower age barriers:** Advocate for broader age ranges in trial protocols; currently many cap at 60–65
- **Caregiver support:** Provide caregiver stipends and on-site support for participants who need assistance with prosthetic management

### 7. Rural Outreach via VA and Federal Health Centers

- **Leverage federal networks:** Partner with VA hospitals and federally qualified health centers (FQHCs) in the rural Midwest and South to identify eligible prosthetic users
- **Mobile prosthetics labs:** Deploy mobile fitting and assessment units to rural locations

### 8. Data Transparency Dashboard

- **Public-facing dashboard:** Create an open-access dashboard showing trial demographics, enrollment status, and geographic coverage
- **Community advisory board:** Establish a board of prosthetic users from underserved communities to advise on trial design and outreach priorities

## Methodology

### Data Sources
- **ClinicalTrials.gov** — Trial recruitment status, sponsor types, phases, enrollment numbers, geographic distribution (as of July 2026)
- **OpenStreetMap** — Geocoding, nearby facilities, transit stops, parking, bicycle infrastructure, EV charging, neighborhood analysis

### Limitations
- ClinicalTrials.gov data reflects publicly listed trials; some large industry trials may be unreported
- OSM data accuracy depends on community mapping completeness; some facilities may be missing
- Site accessibility scores are a snapshot in time; facilities may change
